This pharmacy offers these services to help you with your medicines and appliances (such as a stoma bag or catheter) free on the NHS.

Medicine questions

Pharmacists can answer your questions on prescription-only medicines plus medicines that you can buy without a prescription such as paracetamol.

They can also help with the disposal of unwanted or out of date medicines.

Find out more about commonly prescribed medicines and medicines you can buy without a prescription in our medicines A-Z.

Advice about new medicines

Advice and support to tell you more about a medicine you’ve just begun taking for:

  • asthma
  • chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • type 2 diabetes
  • high blood pressure
  • conditions which need blood thinning medicines

Medicine check-up

A medicine review by a pharmacist if you take several prescription medicines or have a long-term illness. The service focuses on:

  • increasing your knowledge of the medicines you take
  • why you take them
  • how you can take them more easily

Stoma bag fitting

The pharmacist makes sure that your stoma bag fits properly and is comfortable for you to wear.

Advice about appliances

Advice and support from a pharmacist to help you use, look after, change and dispose of an appliance. Examples of appliances are:

  • a stoma bag
  • catheter
  • wound drainage pouch

Get help with NHS prescription costs

There is usually a charge for NHS prescriptions.

You can sometimes get free NHS prescriptions, for example if you’re over 60, or if you’re pregnant.

Prescriptions are also free for children and for people on some benefits.

You should check what help you could get to pay for NHS prescriptions. This is because you may be fined up to £100 plus the cost of your treatment if you claim for free treatment you’re not entitled to.
