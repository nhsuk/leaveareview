There is usually a charge for NHS prescriptions.

You can sometimes get free NHS prescriptions, for example if you are:

  • 60 or over
  • under 16
  • pregnant or have had a baby in the previous 12 months and have a valid maternity exemption certificate
  • on certain benefits

Find out if you’re entitled to free prescriptions

If you pay for lots of prescriptions, you can also save money with a kind of ‘season ticket’ called a prescription prepayment certificate (PPC). Find out more about prescription prepayment certificates.

Check what help you could get to pay for NHS prescriptions.

This is because you may be fined up to £100 plus the cost of your treatment if you claim for free treatment you’re not entitled to.
