GP Patient Survey

The GP Patient Survey is an independent survey run by Ipsos MORI on behalf of the NHS. The survey is sent out to over two million people. The results show how people feel about their GP practice.

Where patient experience is best

  • 98% of respondents felt their needs were met during their last general practice appointment

    Local (CCG) average: 96%
    National average: 94%

  • 97% of respondents were involved as much as they wanted to be in decisions about their care and treatment during their last general practice appointment

    Local (CCG) average: 96%
    National average: 93%

  • 82% of respondents say they have had enough support from local services or organisations in the last 12 months to help manage their long-term condition(s)

    Local (CCG) average: 83%
    National average: 78%

Where patient experience could improve

  • 27% of respondents find it easy to get through to this GP practice by phone

    Local (CCG) average: 69%
    National average: 68%

  • 54% of respondents describe their experience of making an appointment as good

    Local (CCG) average: 72%
    National average: 67%

  • 32% of respondents usually get to see or speak to their preferred GP when they would like to

    Local (CCG) average: 49%
    National average: 48%
