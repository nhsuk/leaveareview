How to book an appointment

You will generally need to be referred by a doctor for a hospital appointment or admission to hospital as an in-patient.

There are 2 ways to get a hospital appointment or admission:

  • we will contact you to arrange a date
  • you will be asked to book your own appointment using the NHS e-Referral Service

The NHS e-Referral service

Your GP may be able to book your appointment with you at the same time as they refer you.

Or you can book your appointment at a later stage. In this instance, you’ll be given an appointment request letter which includes:

  • your unique booking reference number
  • your NHS Number
  • a list of hospitals or clinics to choose from
  • a password

You can use these details to:

  • book online
  • book by phoning The Appointments Line (TAL) on 01908 774 663 (Monday to Friday 9am-5pm, excluding bank holidays)

Find more information about booking an appointment using the NHS e-Referral service.

How to cancel or change an appointment

We understand there may be times when you’re unable to attend your appointment or admission.

If you booked your appointment using the NHS e-Referral service, you can change or cancel the appointment online or by phoning The Appointments Line (TAL) on 01908 774 663 (Monday to Friday 9am-5pm, excluding bank holidays).
