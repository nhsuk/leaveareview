Making a complaint

If you are unhappy with a certain aspect of service or care provided by this hospital, often a useful first step is to talk directly to a member of staff involved. This could be a nurse, your consultant or other specialist. You may find it is possible to sort out the problem straight away.

The Patient Advice and Liaison Service

Another option is to get in touch with this hospital’s Patient Advice and Liaison Service (PALS).

Find out more about this Hospital’s Patient Advice and Liaison Service

Formal complaint

If you are still not satisfied you have the option of making a formal complaint, which should be made in writing to the NHS trust that runs this hospital.

Find out more about how to complain to the NHS.

There are also other ways of complaining about NHS treatment and services, such as complaining to the body which pays for certain NHS services. For hospitals this would be your local clinical commissioning group (CCG).
