How to order repeat prescriptions online

Most prescriptions are now electronic, which means you do not have to go into your GP surgery to get a repeat prescription.

You can order your medicine online and get it delivered to you or collect it from a pharmacy.

You can do this in 3 simple steps.

1. Register with an online prescription service

RP

You can order repeat prescriptions through the NHS website. You will need to register with NHS login to use the service.

Register for repeat prescriptions via the NHS website

There are other apps and websites you can use to order repeat prescriptions. Some may also let you see your medical records and book GP appointments.

You can also:

  • use the NHS app from the Google Play store and the App store
  • use one of the online health services that are available at your GP surgery – check your GP surgery website to see which providers you can use
  • search online for pharmacies that have an online repeat prescription service

When you’ve chosen a service to use, you’ll need to register to create an account.

2. Order your prescription

Once you’ve created an account using the website or app, you will be able to see your existing repeat prescriptions or order new ones.

Your GP may have already approved a number of repeat prescriptions that you can order from a pharmacy. This is called a Repeat Dispense.

Some repeat prescriptions may need approval from your GP before you can order them from a pharmacy.

If you do not have a repeat prescription, a request will be sent for your GP surgery to approve. You’ll be told when the prescription has been approved or if the GP wants to talk to you before they approve it.

3. Choose how to collect your prescription

You have a choice of different ways to collect your prescription

You may be able to:

  • get your medicine delivered to you
  • get your medicine sent to a specific pharmacy (nominated pharmacy) — they'll let you know when it's ready to collect
  • go to any pharmacy to collect your medicine — you may get a paper copy of your prescription to bring with you, or the pharmacy may be able to find your prescription using your NHS number

Do not go to a pharmacy to collect medicines if:

  • you have symptoms of coronavirus (a high temperature or a new, continuous cough)
  • you live with someone who has symptoms
  • you are at very high risk of coronavirus

Get your medicines delivered or ask someone to collect them.

If you're pick up medicines for someone else, leave them outside their door but make sure you see them collect them.
