How to order repeat prescriptions online

Most prescriptions are now electronic, which means you do not have to go into your GP surgery to get a repeat prescription.

You can order your medicine online and get it delivered to you or collect it from a pharmacy.

You can do this in 3 simple steps.

1. Register with an online prescription service

Using this website

The NHS website now allows you to order repeat prescriptions online. To make use of this service you will need to:

  • be registered at a GP surgery in England
  • be aged 13 or older
  • already have a current repeat prescription approved by your GP
Information:

You will also need an NHS login. If you do not have an NHS login then the repeat prescription service will explain how you register for one.

Read more about how you register for the GP repeat prescription service.

Using other services

There are lots of other apps and websites you can use to order repeat prescriptions. Some may also let you see your medical record and book GP appointments.

You can:

  • use the NHS App
  • use another online health service linked to your GP surgery. — check your GP surgery website to see which services you can use
  • search online for pharmacies that have an online repeat prescription service

When you've chosen a service to use, you'll need to register to create an account.

You may be able to:

  • get your medicine delivered to you
  • get your medicine sent to a specific pharmacy (nominated pharmacy) — they'll let you know when it's ready to collect
  • go to any pharmacy to collect your medicine — you may get a paper copy of your prescription to bring with you, or the pharmacy may be able to find your prescription using your NHS number

Do not go to a pharmacy to collect medicines if:

  • you have symptoms of coronavirus (a high temperature or a new, continuous cough)
  • you live with someone who has symptoms
  • you are at very high risk of coronavirus
